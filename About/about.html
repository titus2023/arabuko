Our Story

Arabuko Farm borders Arabuko Sokoke which is the largest Fragment of coastal forest (420 square km) in East Africa. Traditionally. A vast amount of cashew and mango is grown in the area. Horticulture and maize are growing in the hinterland and along The Galana river. In the coastal region, like in many other parts of Kenya, deforestation is taking place at an alarming rate. This is Mostly because of the lucrative charcoal market. The coastal Province of Kenya currently has almost 4 million inhabitants, a high percentage of which are subsistence farmers. Arabuko Farm is heavily intertwined with its community. We hope that our awareness raising through education and training leads to the adoption of a more sustainable business model by local communities. Furthermore, our objective within the community is to integrate Fruit trees into the tree mix so that the value of the trees for the Local population will be higher, as well as sustainable harvesting of Useful forest products such as tree leaves and natural herbs.

Mission

We at Arabuko Farm aim to demonstrate various innovations in horticulture and landscape restoration and- protection. Our Community

In 2020 the land of Arabuko Farm was bought by two agriculture consultants who wanted to put their theory into practice. Their Experience with East African agricultural companies, NGOs, and government agencies showed them the need for knowledge institutions in the form of a demonstration farm. After 2 years of landscape restoration of the degraded land, the first Fruit was being harvested and the regional farmers started to Show interest in the innovative practices at Arabuko Farm.
